# Public Claim Audit

Last checked: 2026-06-08

This note records the public wording we can use for the project, what that wording
does and does not claim, and which similar Bay Area projects we checked.

## Recommended Claim

> To our knowledge, this is the most detailed public interactive 3D
> reconstruction of San Francisco's ancient coastline, combining best-available
> public land elevation, Bay bathymetry, offshore seafloor surveys, and
> sea-level history into one Bay-to-Farallones viewer.

Plain English: the claim is about the public interactive ancient-coastline
experience, not about owning or inventing the best raw elevation data.

```text
safe center of the claim

ancient coastline
        +
3D land and seafloor terrain
        +
Bay-to-Farallones scope
        +
best-available public survey patches
        +
source-quality notes
        =
distinctive public interactive reconstruction
```

## Claim Ladder

| Strength | Wording | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Safest | A public interactive 3D paleo-coastline map of San Francisco Bay, the Golden Gate, the offshore shelf, and the Farallon Islands. | Use freely. |
| Strong | A distinctive public interactive reconstruction of San Francisco's ancient coastline, built from best-available public land and seafloor elevation data. | Good default wording. |
| Stronger | To our knowledge, the most detailed public interactive 3D ancient-coastline reconstruction for the Bay-to-Farallones region. | Use when the context can carry a caveat. |
| Risky | No one has created a single consolidated map of this fidelity. | Avoid unless paired with a narrow definition and evidence. |
| Too broad | The highest-quality land-and-ocean terrain map of the Bay Area. | Avoid. USGS and NOAA publish high-quality raw elevation and bathymetry products. |

## What We Are Not Claiming

We are not claiming that this app is the best raw elevation model of the Bay
Area. Public agencies already publish excellent land-and-water elevation data.

We are not claiming that every source is in one perfect vertical reference. Some
high-detail sources use different zero-height references, so exact scientific
waterline claims still need datum checks.

We are not claiming that no similar map, paper, dataset, or local research
project exists anywhere. The defensible wording is "to our knowledge" because
private, unpublished, or hard-to-index work can always exist.

## Public Wording Rules

Use "to our knowledge" for the strongest version.

Say "public interactive 3D reconstruction" instead of "best map" when possible.

Say "land and seafloor elevation" instead of "land and ocean topology." In plain
English: topography means land height, bathymetry means seafloor depth, and
topobathymetry means both together.

When describing fidelity, point to the app's stitched public sources, source
quality notes, and Bay-to-Farallones scope. Do not imply that the app replaces
official USGS or NOAA elevation products.
